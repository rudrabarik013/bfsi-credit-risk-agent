RESERVE BANK OF INDIA
CREDIT RISK COMPLIANCE GUIDELINES FOR RETAIL LENDING
(Based on RBI Master Circular - Fair Practices Code for Lenders | RBI/2015-16/70)
(Based on RBI Master Direction - Credit Card and Retail Lending Directions, 2022)
(Based on RBI Guidelines on Credit Risk Management for Banks, 2023)

Compiled Reference Document for Internal Compliance Use
================================================================


SECTION 1: BORROWER ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
-----------------------------------------

1.1 AGE REQUIREMENTS
- Minimum age of borrower at loan origination: 21 years
- Maximum age of borrower at loan maturity: 65 years (salaried), 70 years (self-employed)
- Borrowers below 21 years are NOT eligible for retail credit products.
- Borrowers above 60 years must provide succession documentation.
- Note: Age 18-20 may be eligible for microfinance products only, not standard retail credit.

1.2 EMPLOYMENT AND INCOME STABILITY
- Borrowers must demonstrate stable and verifiable income.
- Skilled and Highly Skilled employment categories are preferred for unsecured credit.
- Unskilled (non-resident) borrowers require additional collateral or guarantor.
- Unskilled (resident) borrowers may be eligible for secured loans only.
- Self-employed borrowers must provide 2 years of ITR (Income Tax Returns).
- Minimum employment continuity: 1 year in current job for salaried borrowers.

1.3 HOUSING STABILITY
- Borrowers with own housing are considered LOW risk on residential stability parameter.
- Borrowers in rented accommodation are considered MODERATE risk.
- Borrowers with free accommodation (living with family) require additional income verification.
- Housing type must be consistent with declared income and credit amount requested.


SECTION 2: CREDIT AMOUNT AND DURATION LIMITS
----------------------------------------------

2.1 CREDIT AMOUNT LIMITS BY EMPLOYMENT TYPE
- Highly Skilled borrowers     : Up to 50,000 DM (or equivalent) unsecured
- Skilled borrowers            : Up to 20,000 DM unsecured; up to 40,000 DM with collateral
- Unskilled (resident)         : Up to 5,000 DM with guarantor
- Unskilled (non-resident)     : Maximum 2,000 DM; collateral mandatory

2.2 LOAN DURATION LIMITS
- Minimum loan duration: 6 months
- Maximum loan duration for consumer goods (radio/TV, furniture): 36 months
- Maximum loan duration for car loans: 60 months
- Maximum loan duration for business/repairs: 48 months
- Maximum loan duration for education: 84 months
- Maximum loan duration for domestic appliances: 24 months
- Loans exceeding these duration limits require documented justification.

2.3 CREDIT AMOUNT TO SAVINGS RATIO
- Borrowers with 'little' savings: Credit amount must not exceed 5,000 DM unsecured.
- Borrowers with 'moderate' savings: Credit amount up to 15,000 DM.
- Borrowers with 'quite rich' savings: Credit amount up to 30,000 DM.
- Borrowers with 'rich' savings: Credit amount up to 50,000 DM.
- Any deviation from these thresholds requires senior credit officer approval.

2.4 CHECKING ACCOUNT AND CREDIT ASSESSMENT
- Borrowers with 'little' checking account balance: HIGH risk flag; requires additional review.
- Borrowers with 'moderate' checking account: MODERATE risk; standard underwriting applies.
- Borrowers with 'rich' checking account: LOW risk; expedited processing permitted.
- No checking account (NA): Mandatory branch verification before loan sanction.


SECTION 3: LOAN PURPOSE GUIDELINES
------------------------------------

3.1 APPROVED LOAN PURPOSES AND RISK CLASSIFICATION
- car              : MODERATE risk — asset-backed, standard processing
- furniture        : LOW-MODERATE risk — essential asset, standard processing
- radio/TV         : MODERATE-HIGH risk — depreciating asset, short duration preferred
- domestic appliances: LOW-MODERATE risk — essential, standard processing
- repairs          : LOW risk — property improvement, acceptable collateral
- education        : LOW risk — productive investment, long duration permitted
- business         : MODERATE-HIGH risk — requires business plan and projections
- vacation         : HIGH risk — no asset backing, strict scrutiny required
- retraining       : LOW-MODERATE risk — productive investment

3.2 PURPOSE-SPECIFIC COMPLIANCE FLAGS
- Radio/TV loans exceeding 36 months duration: FLAG for senior review.
- Vacation loans exceeding 12 months duration: REJECT automatically.
- Business loans must have documented business plan on file.
- Education loans for borrowers below 21: Require co-borrower (parent/guardian).


SECTION 4: RISK CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK
------------------------------------------

4.1 OVERALL RISK SCORING (INTERNAL USE)
Banks must classify retail borrowers into one of three categories:

GOOD RISK (Low Default Probability):
- Age: 30-55 years
- Employment: Skilled or Highly Skilled
- Housing: Own
- Savings: Moderate, Quite Rich, or Rich
- Checking Account: Moderate or Rich
- Credit Amount: Within savings-ratio limits
- Duration: Within purpose-specific limits
- Purpose: Education, repairs, car, furniture, domestic appliances

BAD RISK (High Default Probability):
- Age: Below 25 or above 60
- Employment: Unskilled (resident or non-resident)
- Housing: Rent or Free
- Savings: Little or NA
- Checking Account: Little or NA
- Credit Amount: Exceeds savings-ratio thresholds
- Duration: Exceeds purpose-specific limits
- Purpose: Vacation, business (without plan)

MODERATE RISK:
- Borrowers who do not clearly fall into GOOD or BAD categories.
- Mixed profile indicators requiring holistic assessment.


SECTION 5: FAIR PRACTICES CODE — MANDATORY OBLIGATIONS
---------------------------------------------------------

5.1 TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENTS (RBI/2015-16/70, Para 3)
- Lenders must clearly communicate the basis for credit decisions to applicants.
- Rejection reasons must be communicated in writing within 30 days.
- Interest rates and all charges must be disclosed upfront before loan sanction.
- No hidden charges are permitted.

5.2 NON-DISCRIMINATION POLICY (RBI Master Circular, Para 5)
- Credit decisions must NOT be influenced by borrower's sex/gender.
- Credit decisions must NOT be based on religion, caste, or community.
- Only financial and credit-risk-relevant parameters may be used in assessment.
- Sex/Gender field in applicant profile must NOT be used as a risk factor.

5.3 DATA PRIVACY AND DOCUMENTATION
- All applicant data must be stored securely per RBI cybersecurity guidelines.
- KYC documentation must be collected and verified before loan disbursement.
- Loan files must be retained for a minimum of 8 years post loan closure.

5.4 GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
- All lenders must have a documented grievance redressal mechanism.
- Customer complaints must be resolved within 30 days.
- Escalation to Banking Ombudsman is available to customers if unresolved.


SECTION 6: COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST FOR CREDIT OFFICERS
------------------------------------------------------

Before sanctioning any retail loan, the following must be verified:

[ ] Borrower age is between 21 and 65 years (salaried) or 70 years (self-employed)
[ ] Employment type is documented and verified
[ ] Credit amount is within the savings-ratio threshold for borrower's savings category
[ ] Loan duration does not exceed the purpose-specific maximum
[ ] Housing status is documented
[ ] Checking account status is verified
[ ] Loan purpose is an approved category
[ ] Non-discrimination compliance confirmed (sex/gender not used as risk factor)
[ ] KYC documentation collected
[ ] Interest rate and charges disclosed in writing


SECTION 7: REGULATORY PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
-----------------------------------------------------

- Failure to disclose interest rates: Fine up to INR 1 Crore per instance
- Discriminatory lending practices: License suspension risk
- Exceeding approved credit limits without documentation: Regulatory censure
- Failure to resolve customer complaints: Fine up to INR 50 Lakhs
- Data privacy breach: Action under IT Act + RBI penalties
